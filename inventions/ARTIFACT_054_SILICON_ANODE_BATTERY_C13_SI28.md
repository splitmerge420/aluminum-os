### Why Silicon Anodes Matter

Conventional lithium-ion batteries use graphite anodes. Silicon anodes have ~10x higher theoretical energy density than graphite — but silicon expands ~300% during charging, causing cracking and failure after relatively few cycles. This is the core engineering challenge blocking silicon anode commercialization for decades.

### Dave's Thesis (C13/Si28 Anode)

- **Si28 isotopic purity** reduces phonon scattering and improves structural uniformity — potentially reducing the cracking/swelling failure mode
- **C13 in the composite** provides a carbon matrix that accommodates silicon volume expansion
- **The 50-50 C13/Si28 ratio** (Sheldonium) may provide the buffering structure that pure silicon anodes lack
- **If validated:** orders-of-magnitude improvement in cycle life for high-energy-density silicon anode batteries
