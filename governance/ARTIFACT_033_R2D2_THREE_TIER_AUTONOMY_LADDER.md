## The Three-Tier Autonomy Ladder

### Tier 1 — Full Autonomy Permitted (Anti-Material Defensive Intercept)
- Interception of unmanned inbound threats: ballistic missiles, cruise missiles, UAVs, loitering munitions (Shahed-136, Lancet, Switchblade), hypersonic glide vehicles
- These are hardware-vs-hardware engagements. They do not constitute lethal targeting of human personnel under LOAC
- Human HITL is **upstream only**: approval of engagement envelopes, ROE, defended asset list
- No per-shot human confirmation required — physically impossible and operationally unnecessary
- Systems: Iron Dome, CIWS, Patriot, THAAD, Aegis

### Tier 2 — Supervised Autonomy (Edge Cases with Manned Platform Risk)
- Same systems when the engagement envelope has been pre-classified by JAG review as potentially involving manned platforms
- **CRITICAL (Claude addendum):** The "plausibly manned" determination must be defined *a priori* in the JAG-reviewed engagement envelope — NOT assessed in-flight by the AI system. Any scenario not explicitly pre-classified as Tier 1 defaults to Tier 3.
- AI provides R2D2-level identification and recommendation; human can override

### Tier 3 — Strict HITL (All Offensive Targeting of Human Beings)
- Every offensive kill decision: AI provides targeting recommendation, confidence score, civilian risk probability estimate
- Qualified, trained human must affirmatively authorize the shot under rules of engagement
- AI never autonomously selects or executes offensive lethal targeting
- All liability for offensive lethal action sits with the authorizing human under command responsibility doctrine

## Contract Addendum v1.1

### Section 2 — Prohibited Uses
Contractor AI may not autonomously select, authorize, or execute offensive lethal targeting of human beings. This prohibition is **absolute and non-waivable**.

### Section 3 — Permitted Defensive Autonomy (Tier 1)
Contractor AI may operate fully autonomously in Tier 1 defensive intercepts: destruction of unmanned inbound munitions. These engagements are anti-material only and do not constitute lethal targeting of human personnel under LOAC.

All such operations shall remain strictly within pre-approved, JAG-reviewed engagement envelopes. AI systems do not make in-flight determinations about target human/non-human status. Any ambiguous or unclassified scenario defaults to Tier 3.

### Section 4 — R2D2 Protocol (All Tiers)
In all Tier 2 and Tier 3 engagements, Contractor AI functions as:
- Perception and sensor fusion layer
- Threat identification with confidence scores
- Targeting recommendations with civilian risk probability
- Situational awareness and telemetry
- **Decision support, never decision authority**

### Section 6 — Audit and Compliance
All Tier 2 and Tier 3 engagement decisions are logged with cryptographic provenance. Engagement logs are available for Article 36 review, JAG review, and congressional oversight upon lawful request.
