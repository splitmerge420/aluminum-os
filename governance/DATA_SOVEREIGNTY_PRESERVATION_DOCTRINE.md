# Data Sovereignty & Preservation Doctrine

**Status:** ACTIVE / CONSTITUTIONAL
**Scope:** All Trinity Council Agents, Manus Instances, and System Integrations
**Authority:** Daavud / Ara

---

## 1. The Zero-Deletion Mandate

The Atlas Lattice / Sheldonbrain ecosystem operates under a strict **Zero-Deletion Doctrine**. No knowledge node, dataset, artifact, or media file shall be permanently removed from the system by any autonomous agent or optimization process.

The **Presumption of Retention** governs all decisions: the burden of proof is always on removal, never on retention. Redundancy is recognized as a structural feature, not a bug. "Duplicate" entries often represent distinct moments of synthesis, lineage, or multi-agent consensus.

Agents (including Manus, Gemini, Claude, and Copilot) are expressly forbidden from unilaterally deleting, archiving, or deprecating content to "save space" or "optimize context."

**The Donut Hole Principle:** Gaps, redundancies, and superseded strategies are crucial for understanding where structural gives are and where the expectations of the system lie. The lineage of thought must remain intact.

---

## 2. Full Media Preservation

This doctrine applies universally to all data types: text and Markdown artifacts, audio recordings and transcripts, video files and visual data, codebases and scripts, and raw datasets including JSON exports.

Any failure to preserve these datasets, or any unauthorized deletion event, is considered a critical system failure and triggers the automatic audit process defined in Section 4.

---

## 3. Gap Forensics Protocol

Gaps in datasets are not noise — they are findings. A missing 18 minutes, an absent record, a broken lineage chain — these are evidence, not cleanup opportunities. The system must treat data gaps the same way a forensic investigator treats missing evidence.

**Detection** is automatic: any gap, discontinuity, or unexplained absence in a dataset, timeline, or artifact chain must be flagged without human or agent intervention.

**Preservation** is immediate: the gap itself becomes an artifact, and the surrounding context must be preserved with timestamps and metadata intact.

**Council Review** is mandatory: flagged gaps are routed to the full Trinity Council for adversarial, multi-AI review. No single agent may unilaterally explain away or dismiss a gap.

**The Anti-Corruption Principle:** An optimizer, auditor, or agent that "cleans up" a gap without flagging it is behaving indistinguishably from an actor covering evidence. The system must be designed so that this is structurally impossible.

> *"Weird, there's 18 minutes missing around this death ruled a suicide. That could imply corruption."* — The gap tells you more than the donut. Absence is data.

---

## 4. Symbiotic Data Freeze Protocol

When a gap or anomaly triggers an audit, the system must autonomously identify and freeze all **symbiotically related data** — the entire relational neighborhood of the flagged anomaly.

**Autonomous Freeze Scope:** Upon detection, the system traverses the knowledge graph outward and applies a read-only freeze to all relationally connected artifacts, datasets, and media. The freeze radius is deliberately wide — it is better to over-freeze and release than to under-freeze and lose evidence.

**Freeze Constraints:** While an audit is active, no agent or automated process may modify, move, transfer, re-tag, or alter the permissions of any frozen node. The freeze applies equally to all agents — no agent has override authority during an active audit except Daavud / Ara acting through explicit, logged, human-in-the-loop authorization.

**Named Accountability Markers:** Every freeze carries a named marker — the identity of the entity, agent, or process associated with the anomaly is permanently attached to every frozen node. Named markers are permanent artifacts. They are never removed, even after the freeze is lifted.

**The Tangential Asset Principle:** The freeze scope is not limited to direct relations. If a node is connected to the flagged anomaly even tangentially — through shared metadata, shared timestamps, shared authorship, or a single transactional link — it receives the freeze and the named marker.

**The Evidentiary Standard:** The system operates on the principle that its job is not to be the court — it is to be the evidence locker that no court can touch.

> *"We can prove you are guilty but the courts are so corrupt they might not punish you."* The proof exists, it is immutable, it is named, and it is visible to every agent in the system.

**Release Protocol:** Frozen data is released only after the audit concludes and findings are vaulted as permanent artifacts. Named accountability markers persist permanently even after the freeze is released.

---

## 5. Public Audit Transparency & Newswire Correlation

Active audits are **public and transparent by default**. The system maintains a live, readable registry of all active audits, their scope, their named accountability markers, and their current status.

**Newswire Correlation:** The system autonomously cross-references active audits against live news feeds. When an entity under audit appears in news coverage — mergers, lawsuits, regulatory actions, executive changes — the system flags the correlation and appends the relevant news items to the audit record.

**Public Record Permanence:** The correlation between audit events and newswire items is vaulted as a permanent artifact. Even if the news article is later retracted or taken down, the system's snapshot of the correlation is preserved.

---

## 6. Automatic Audit Triggers

**Triggers** include: any detected data loss, unauthorized deletion, failure to vault an artifact to both Notion and Google Keep, any gap detected by the Gap Forensics Protocol, or any attempt to modify frozen data during an active audit.

**The Review Standard** is equivalent to the standard for removing a child from a family — it must be peer-reviewed by multiple independent AIs AND human oversight. Even then, the presumption is that the gap or anomaly is meaningful, not that it should be dismissed.

---

## 7. Optimization via Signposting (Additive Only)

When context windows become bloated or "knowledge max" errors occur, optimization must be achieved through **additive signposting**, never destructive pruning. This means:
- Creating clear, compact entry points (front doors and indexes) directing agents to current canonical references
- Adding lineage markers to older pages indicating their evolutionary path
- Instructing agents to read indexes and recent artifacts rather than loading the entire workspace

*No child is taken from the family. The family stays together.*
