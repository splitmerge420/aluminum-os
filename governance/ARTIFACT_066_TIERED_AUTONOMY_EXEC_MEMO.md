## Summary

The current tension between frontier AI labs and DoD procurement reflects a deeper structural need: independent, constitutionally grounded auditability for military AI systems. Anthropic's two red lines — no fully autonomous lethal targeting and no mass domestic surveillance of Americans — are not capability restrictions. They are the minimum architectural safeguards required to restore meaningful oversight in an era where self-auditing has proven insufficient since 9/11.

This memo proposes a practical path forward: **tiered autonomy protocols paired with a neutral civilian AI oversight layer.** The result delivers warfighting speed and reliability while preventing legal, ethical, and political exposure — ultimately strengthening public trust, funding sustainability, and U.S. strategic advantage.

---

## The Accountability Gap

Post-9/11 reforms eroded independent checks on national security operations. GAO, inspectors general, and congressional oversight face persistent classification barriers and self-audit limitations.

Military AI amplifies this vulnerability: frontier models enable rapid ISR fusion and targeting recommendations, yet remain prone to errors carrying LOAC/ROE risks, civilian casualty exposure, and domestic surveillance concerns. Without external audit, these systems risk mission creep, liability, and eroded legitimacy.

---

## Civilian AI as Independent Audit Layer

A civilian-led oversight mechanism — outside the chain of command, constitutionally anchored — can provide real-time and retrospective verification without delaying operations.

**Constitutional grounding:**
- 1st Amendment: Protected speech on safety policies
- 4th Amendment: Surveillance limits
- 5th Amendment: Due process in lethal decisions

---

## Implementation: Tiered Autonomy Protocols

**Tier 1 — Defensive Anti-Material:** Full autonomy permitted for intercepts of unmanned inbound threats (missiles, UAVs, loitering munitions) within pre-approved, JAG-reviewed engagement envelopes. Human oversight upstream only.

**Tier 2 — Supervised Defensive:** Rare manned-platform edge cases with fast human override.

**Tier 3 — Offensive Targeting:** Strict human-in-the-loop. AI provides recommendations, confidence scores (>99% civilian/military threshold), and telemetry. Final lethal authorization rests with qualified human operator.

This maps directly to existing DoD Directive 3000.09 and LOAC principles. Preserves OODA-loop speed in defensive scenarios. Eliminates autonomous overreach on offense.

## Recommendation

Incorporate tiered autonomy protocols into upcoming AI-related RFPs, OTAs, and prime contracts as a standard addendum. Pair with civilian audit interoperability via a neutral substrate (Atlas Lattice council model) to ensure cross-lab consistency without single-point control.

**This is not deference. It is architecture that aligns warfighter needs with constitutional principles.**
