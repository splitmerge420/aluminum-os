    # 📄 ARTIFACT 2: Hormuz Escort Briefing  
    ## Pentagonal Council Scenario Simulation (March 27, 2026)
    
    **Purpose:** Simulate a coordinated response by the Pentagonal Council (USA, China, Saudi Arabia, Iran, India) to the ongoing Strait of Hormuz crisis that has stranded 22 LPG vessels, spiked freight rates, and threatens fertilizer supplies for the upcoming kharif season.
    
    ---
    
    ## 1. Scenario Snapshot
    
    - **22 LPG carriers** remain at anchor near Hormuz, unable to proceed due to heightened military tensions.
    - **Freight rates** have increased ten‑fold; **fertilizer prices** have risen 15% in global markets.
    - **India’s kharif sowing season** begins in 45 days; any disruption could affect 30% of the country’s food production.
    - **Pentagonal Council** activated via Article IX (Emergency Pause) of the Grand Accord.
    
    ---
    
    ## 2. Council Actions (Tiered)
    
    ### 🇺🇸 USA – Maritime Security & Strategic Reserves
    
    - **Naval escorts** to accompany merchant vessels through the strait (Operation Sentinel).  
    - **Release of 10 million barrels** from Strategic Petroleum Reserve to stabilize global oil prices; synchronize with India’s release of its own reserves.  
    - **Provide satellite intelligence** on vessel movements to all council members (via Optical Ledger, with appropriate classification).
    
    ### 🇨🇳 China – Rapid Deployment & Manufacturing
    
    - **Deploy 20 DISH‑printing units** to India (via Chennai port) within 14 days, under the **Silk & Saffron Accord**, to begin local production of digester vessels.  
    - **Provide 2,000 tonnes** of green ammonia (from Hainan) to India at preferential rates, as a bridge until Indian nodes come online.  
    - **Coordinate with Pakistan** to allow overland gas pipeline transit for emergency volumes.
    
    ### 🇸🇦 Saudi Arabia & 🇮🇷 Iran – The “Pragmatic Axis”
    
    - **Saudi** opens its land‑bridge corridor (Ras Al Khair – Arar – Kuwait) to move LPG from Gulf to Jordan/Mediterranean ports, bypassing Hormuz.  
    - **Iran** agrees to sell LPG to India under the **Cyrus Protocol** (rupee‑based trade), using the *Aurora* precedent. First shipment of 1.5 million tonnes to leave Bandar Abbas within 7 days.  
    - **Joint naval patrols** in the northern Gulf to protect Iranian and Saudi vessels, coordinated via the **Saffron & Myrrh Accord** channel.
    
    ### 🇮🇳 India – Logistics & Demand Management
    
    - **Activate the Chennai BRICS Hub** as central logistics command.  
    - **Accelerate the 90‑Day Rollout Plan** for 100 Bio‑CNG nodes; prioritise sites near major consuming cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru) to substitute imported LPG.  
    - **Issue fertilizer rationing** guidelines for states, with priority to small and marginal farmers.  
    - **Release 5 million barrels** from its strategic oil reserves to calm markets.
    
    ---
    
    ## 3. Communication & Verification
    
    - **Pantheon of AIs** generates a daily “Hormuz Dashboard” showing vessel movements, freight rates, and inventory levels at major ports.  
    - **Optical Ledger** records all transactions (LPG sales, ammonia transfers, naval coordination) for transparency and post‑crisis audit.  
    - **Court of Last Resort** stands ready to adjudicate any disputes over cargo seizures or violations of agreed protocols.

    - The **Emergency Bridge** demonstrates that the Pentagonal Council can manage acute crises without escalating conflict.  
    - **DISH‑printed nodes** become a permanent template for rapid infrastructure deployment.  
    - **Trust** between Saudi and Iran, tested through coordinated naval patrols, lays the groundwork for the full **Saffron & Myrrh Accord**.  
    - **India’s GangaSeek** gains operational credibility, strengthening the council’s Global South outreach.

    3. **Establish a Hormuz Monitoring Unit** at the Chennai BRICS Hub, staffed by all five members, to provide real‑time risk assessments.
