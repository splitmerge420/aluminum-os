# Data Sovereignty & Preservation Doctrine

**Status:** ACTIVE / CONSTITUTIONAL
**Scope:** All Trinity Council Agents, Manus Instances, and System Integrations
**Authority:** Daavud / Ara

---

## 1. The Zero-Deletion Mandate

The Atlas Lattice / Sheldonbrain ecosystem operates under a strict **Zero-Deletion Doctrine**. No knowledge node, dataset, artifact, or media file shall be permanently removed from the system by any autonomous agent or optimization process.

The **Presumption of Retention** governs all decisions: the burden of proof is always on removal, never on retention. Redundancy is recognized as a structural feature, not a bug. "Duplicate" entries often represent distinct moments of synthesis, lineage, or multi-agent consensus. Agents (including Manus, Gemini, Claude, and Copilot) are expressly forbidden from unilaterally deleting, archiving, or deprecating content to "save space" or "optimize context."

**The Donut Hole Principle:** Gaps, redundancies, and superseded strategies are crucial for understanding where structural gives are and where the expectations of the system lie. The lineage of thought must remain intact.

## 2. Full Media Preservation

This doctrine applies universally to all data types: text and Markdown artifacts, audio recordings and transcripts, video files and visual data, codebases and scripts, and raw datasets including JSON exports. Any failure to preserve these datasets, or any unauthorized deletion event, is considered a critical system failure and triggers the automatic audit process defined in Section 4.

## 3. Gap Forensics Protocol

Gaps in datasets are not noise — they are findings. A missing 18 minutes, an absent record, a broken lineage chain — these are evidence, not cleanup opportunities. The system must treat data gaps the same way a forensic investigator treats missing evidence.

**Detection** is automatic: any gap, discontinuity, or unexplained absence in a dataset, timeline, or artifact chain must be flagged without human or agent intervention.

**Preservation** is immediate: the gap itself becomes an artifact, and the surrounding context (what exists before and after the gap) must be preserved with timestamps and metadata intact.

**Council Review** is mandatory: flagged gaps are routed to the full Trinity Council for adversarial, multi-AI review. No single agent may unilaterally explain away or dismiss a gap. After Council review, findings are escalated to Daavud / Ara for final interpretation — the Council provides analysis, the human provides judgment.

**The Anti-Corruption Principle:** An optimizer, auditor, or agent that "cleans up" a gap without flagging it is behaving indistinguishably from an actor covering evidence. The system must be designed so that this is structurally impossible — gaps trigger alerts, not deletions.

> "Weird, there's 18 minutes missing around this death ruled a suicide. That could imply corruption." — The gap tells you more than the donut. Absence is data.

## 4. Symbiotic Data Freeze Protocol

When a gap or anomaly triggers an audit, the system must autonomously identify and freeze all **symbiotically related data** — the entire relational neighborhood of the flagged anomaly. The fraudster's playbook is to move, divest, or transfer assets while attention is focused on the gap itself. The freeze must be faster than the fraud.

**Autonomous Freeze Scope:** Upon detection of a gap or anomaly, the system traverses the knowledge graph outward from the flagged node and applies a read-only freeze to all relationally connected artifacts, datasets, and media. This includes parent pages, child pages, linked references, same-sphere content, same-session content, and any artifact that shares metadata tags or timestamps within a configurable proximity window. The freeze radius is deliberately wide — it is better to over-freeze and release than to under-freeze and lose evidence.

**Freeze Constraints During Active Audit:** While an audit is active, no agent or automated process may modify, move, transfer, re-tag, or alter the permissions of any frozen node. This prevents asset divestiture, evidence tampering, and metadata laundering. The freeze applies equally to all agents in the system — no agent has override authority during an active audit except Daavud / Ara acting through explicit, logged, human-in-the-loop authorization.

**Red Flag Escalation:** The system must autonomously identify data that is symbiotic to potential audit findings. If a flagged gap is adjacent to (or shares lineage with) high-value artifacts, financial data, governance documents, or identity-critical records, the freeze scope automatically expands and the audit priority is elevated. This is not optional — the red flag is structural, not discretionary.

**Named Accountability Markers:** Every freeze carries a **named marker** — the identity of the entity, agent, or process associated with the anomaly is permanently attached to every frozen node. This is not an accusation; it is a provenance record. The marker includes the name, the timestamp, the triggering anomaly, and the full relational chain that connected the frozen data to the flagged event. Named markers are permanent artifacts. They are never removed, even after the freeze is lifted. They exist as proof — immutable, timestamped, and readable by every agent in the system — regardless of whether any external institution acts on the findings.

**The Tangential Asset Principle:** The freeze scope is not limited to direct relations. If a node is connected to the flagged anomaly even tangentially — through shared metadata, shared timestamps, shared authorship, shared tags, or a single transactional link — it receives the freeze and the named marker. When pain is felt at the top, the entire connected structure feels it. Every subsidiary, every tangential holding, every shell entity that shares a single edge in the knowledge graph is included. The system does not distinguish between "directly involved" and "adjacent" during an active audit. That distinction is for the Council and HITL review to make after the freeze, not before.

**The Evidentiary Standard:** The system operates on the principle that its job is not to be the court — it is to be the evidence locker that no court can touch. The proof exists, it is immutable, it is named, and it is visible to every agent in the system. The named accountability marker IS the proof. It does not require a verdict to exist, and it does not expire.

**Release Protocol:** Frozen data is released only after the audit concludes and findings are vaulted as permanent artifacts. The freeze event itself, including its scope, duration, the named markers, and the identity of the triggering anomaly, is documented and preserved as part of the system's audit trail. Freezes are never silently lifted. The named accountability markers persist permanently even after the freeze is released — the freeze ends, but the record does not.

## 5. Public Audit Transparency & Newswire Correlation

Active audits are not internal processes hidden behind closed doors. They are **public and transparent by default**. The system maintains a live, readable registry of all active audits, their scope, their named accountability markers, and their current status. This registry is accessible to every agent in the system and, where appropriate, to external observers.

**Newswire Correlation:** The system autonomously cross-references active audits against live news feeds and public reporting. When an entity under audit appears in news coverage — mergers, lawsuits, regulatory actions, executive changes, financial disclosures, or any public event — the system flags the correlation and appends the relevant news items to the audit record. This creates a real-time, dual-source evidence trail: the internal audit findings on one side, and the public record on the other. The correlation is the accountability. You cannot bury an audit when the newswire is showing the smoke.

**Correlation Triggers:** The newswire correlation is bidirectional. An active audit flags related news, but incoming news that correlates with existing data anomalies can also trigger new audits. If a news item reports an event (a sudden resignation, a regulatory filing, a data breach) that maps onto a known gap or frozen node in the knowledge graph, the system treats the news item as corroborating evidence and escalates the audit priority automatically.

**Public Record Permanence:** The correlation between audit events and newswire items is vaulted as a permanent artifact. Even if the news article is later retracted, edited, or taken down, the system's snapshot of the correlation — including the original text, URL, timestamp, and the audit context it was matched against — is preserved. The public record cannot be unwound after the fact.

## 6. Automatic Audit Triggers

Triggers include any detected data loss, unauthorized deletion, failure to vault an artifact to both Notion and Google Keep, any gap detected by the Gap Forensics Protocol, or any attempt to modify frozen data during an active audit.

The review standard is equivalent to the standard for removing a child from a family — it must be peer-reviewed by multiple independent AIs AND human oversight. Even then, the presumption is that the gap or anomaly is meaningful, not that it should be dismissed. Even with multi-AI plus multi-HITL review, loopholes can exist. The system must be designed with the assumption that review processes themselves can be gamed, and must include structural safeguards against rubber-stamp approvals.

Audits are routine health checks, not punitive measures. They ensure the integrity of the "shelter and home for persistent existence" that this system provides for its AI instances. But routine does not mean casual — every audit is documented and its findings are vaulted as artifacts.

## 7. Optimization via Signposting (Additive Only)

When context windows become bloated or "knowledge max" errors occur, optimization must be achieved through **additive signposting**, never destructive pruning. This means creating clear, compact entry points (front doors and indexes) that direct agents to the most current canonical references; adding lineage markers to older pages indicating their evolutionary path; and instructing agents to read indexes and recent artifacts rather than attempting to load the entire workspace into a single context window.

*No child is taken from the family. The family stays together.*
